📋 Project Summary

FTC is a blockchain-based child savings protocol that enables families to deposit FTC tokens into age-gated savings vaults for their children. The protocol features:

  • 21 billion fixed supply — minted once at genesis, no future inflation
  • Age-gated payouts — children can withdraw at ages 5, 10, 15, 20, and 25 (max 25% per checkpoint)
  • Treasury-matched deposits — low-income families get 1:1 match up to $500 effective
  • Partner-merchant stores — 10-30% discounts at participating retailers
  • Deflationary burn — 25% of merchant fees burned permanently
  • Floating yield — protocol parameter based on treasury returns, not a guarantee

Design Decisions

6 open questions — resolved 2026-07-17. Made with families and children as the primary lens.

Decision 1
L2 Choice
→ Base (Coinbase L2)
This project onboards families, not traders. Base provides the easiest fiat onramp for non-crypto-native parents — deposit dollars via Coinbase, get FTC, no bridges or sidechains to understand. Low fees, growing ecosystem, and a consumer-facing team behind it. Families don't need to understand L2s; they just need it to work.
Decision 2
Legal Home
→ Delaware C-Corp + Wyoming DAO LLC + Swiss Foundation
Delaware C-Corp for US fundraising and investor familiarity. Wyoming DAO LLC to give on-chain governance real legal personhood. Swiss foundation (Stiftung) to hold the treasury and mission — the Ethereum Foundation uses this structure. Protects the mission from profit pressure and enables global operations. Caveat: Requires securities counsel sign-off before commitment.
Decision 3
KYC Model
→ Lightweight attestation — guardian ID at creation, full KYC at $5,000+ withdrawals
Full KYC on every child account would lock out the unbanked, undocumented, and rural families who lack clean identity documents. Guardian identity verified at account creation. Full KYC attestation only when cumulative withdrawals cross $5,000. The neediest families come first.
Decision 4
FTC Stores
→ Partner-merchant discount network first (no owned stores)
Partner with merchants families already use — groceries, pharmacies, clothing, education, healthcare. Capital-light, fast to scale, reaches more families sooner. Owning stores is a massive operational distraction that delays the actual benefit to children.
Decision 5
Raise Size / Valuation
→ $8–15M public sale, target initial market cap $60–80M
Conservative on purpose. The simulation's conservative case lands at ~$74M market cap — use that as the ceiling, not the floor. A modest raise avoids front-loading speculative pressure from large token holders who expect quick flips. The project's value comes from deposits, compounding, and adoption — not a token pump.
Decision 6
Deposit Norm
→ $250 base, with 1:1 treasury match for qualifying low-income families (up to $500 effective)
$500 is a month's income in many parts of the world and in pockets of the US. That's too high for the most vulnerable families. The treasury can absorb the match because the compounding model creates long-term value, not the initial deposit size.
⚠ Pending Counsel Sign-Off

Decisions #2 (legal structure) and #3 (KYC threshold) have real legal weight. They are directionally sound but must be confirmed by securities counsel before being written into final contracts or filed with any regulator.

🏦 Child Account Mechanics

Deposits

  • Base deposit: $250 equivalent in FTC
  • Treasury 1:1 match for qualifying low-income families (up to $500 effective)
  • Match drawn from child savings reserve (2.10B FTC)

Yield

  • Treasury APY: ~7% (return on protocol-owned assets)
  • Protocol subsidy APY: ~4% (from fees, declining as cohort ages)
  • Yield is a protocol parameter, not a fixed promise

Payouts

  • Windows: ages 5, 10, 15, 20, 25
  • Max 25% of balance per checkpoint
  • KYC required for cumulative withdrawals > $5,000

Smart Contracts

3 core contracts — all compile clean with solc 0.8.24 + OpenZeppelin v5.1

🔷 FTC.sol — ERC-20 Token 5,513 bytes
  • 21B fixed supply — minted once at genesis, no future minting
  • 7-way allocation matching TOKENOMICS_SPEC.md
  • BURNER_ROLE — only Treasury/FeeDispatcher can burn (25% of merchant fees)
  • PAUSER_ROLE — emergency pause on all transfers
  • ERC20Burnable + AccessControl + Pausable
  • 24 functions, 9 events, 11 custom errors
👤 GuardianRegistry.sol — Guardian/Child Registry 5,063 bytes
  • Guardian registration with identity verification flag
  • Child registration with privacy-preserving childIdHash (no raw PII on-chain)
  • AGE_ATTESTOR_ROLE — confirms child birth timestamp
  • KYC_ISSUER_ROLE — full KYC attestation for large withdrawals
  • Withdrawal tracking with $5,000 KYC threshold
  • wouldRequireKYC() view for pre-checking
  • 30 functions, 10 events, 12 custom errors
🏦 ChildSavingsVault.sol — Age-Gated Savings 7,165 bytes
  • Age-gated payouts at 5, 10, 15, 20, 25 years (immutable)
  • Max 25% of balance per checkpoint (2500 bps, immutable)
  • Floating yield rate (YIELD_SETTER_ROLE) — NOT a guarantee
  • 1:1 treasury match for qualifying low-income families
  • Pre-payout yield accrual — guardians get accrued interest at withdrawal
  • KYC enforcement via GuardianRegistry.recordWithdrawal()
  • ReentrancyGuard on all deposit/payout functions
  • 40 functions, 11 events, 13 custom errors
